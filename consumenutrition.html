Morning: First Hour

When you wake up, your body is dehydrated after a night of rest. Start your day by drinking a glass of water to rehydrate. If you feel hungry, opt for a light meal such as a piece of fruit, a handful of nuts, or a small smoothie. Avoid heavy meals to allow your body to wake up gradually.

Mid-Morning: Fuel for the Day

By mid-morning, your body needs energy to keep you focused and productive. Choose a balanced snack that includes protein, healthy fats, and complex carbohydrates. Examples include Greek yogurt with granola, a boiled egg with whole-grain toast, or a handful of trail mix.

Late Morning: Fruit and Hydration

As lunchtime approaches, keep your energy levels steady with a piece of fruit and more water. Fruits like apples, oranges, or bananas provide natural sugars and fiber to keep you going. Pair this with a glass of water or herbal tea to stay hydrated.

Lunch: A Balanced Meal

Lunch is an important meal to refuel your body for the rest of the day. Aim for a balanced plate that includes lean protein (like chicken, fish, or tofu), whole grains (like quinoa or brown rice), and plenty of vegetables. Add a healthy fat source, such as avocado or olive oil, for sustained energy.

Dinner: A Light and Nourishing Meal

Dinner should be satisfying but not overly heavy. Focus on lean protein, steamed or roasted vegetables, and a small portion of whole grains or starchy vegetables like sweet potatoes. Avoid large portions or heavy sauces that may disrupt digestion and sleep.

Evening: Foods for Relaxation

In the evening, choose foods that promote relaxation and prepare your body for sleep. Examples include a small bowl of oatmeal with a drizzle of honey, a banana, or a cup of chamomile tea. Avoid caffeine, sugar, and large meals close to bedtime to ensure restful sleep.
